\chapter{Latent Space Analysis}

In classical machine learning approaches, practitioners had to manually design data representations in order to make them usable by simple models.
Deep learning introduces a paradigm shift: rather than relying on hand-crafted features to meaningfully represent data, it automatically learns these representations.
Through the successive activation of their hidden layers, neural networks gradually learn increasingly relevant representations for the task.
As discussed in Chapter~\ref{chap:deep_learning} these representations are not meaningful at initialization; they emerge progressively during training in order to optimize the model’s objective.
It is precisely thanks to these representations that a trained network is able to map an input to an output, even for examples never seen during training.
The study of these representations is therefore a step toward better understanding the internal mechanisms of neural networks.
In this chapter, we first aim to make explicit the research hypotheses commonly shared across most work in this field.
To do so, we will introduce the notions of abstraction, concept, latent space, and semantics.
Once this conceptual framework has been clarified, we will focus on the different methods that attempt to extract concepts from latent spaces.

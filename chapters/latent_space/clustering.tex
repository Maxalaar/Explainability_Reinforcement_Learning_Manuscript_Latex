\section{Clustering}

To overcome the limitations of linear concept assumptions, we adopt a \emphasize{Deep Clustering} approach \cite{ren2022deepclusteringcomprehensivesurvey, WEI2024127761}.
This line of research identifies semantics in the \latentSpaceAcronym by applying clustering functions directly within it.
The resulting clusters correspond to groups of inputs that share a common semantic meaning.
For example, autoencoders can be used to group images with similar semantics without requiring supervision.

This method enables the identification of semantic structures in the \latentSpaceAcronym in an unsupervised manner, without relying on the assumption of linear concept encoding. For this reason, deep clustering is particularly well suited to our explainable reinforcement learning approach.
Building on this line of work, we introduce, \emphasize{\methodName} (\methodeAcronym) method, which is described in the following section.
The main objective is to study how the policy’s \latentSpaceAcronym is organized.
To this end, we aim to identify the semantic clusters within the \latentSpaceAcronym and to investigate their associated meaning using automated techniques.
This approach allows us to identify which states are perceived as similar by the policy and to simplify the underlying Markov decision process for the purpose of enhancing interpretability.

To investigate this, \emphasize{Probing Methods} were developed.
Probing techniques evaluate whether \neuralNetworkAcronym encode specific human-interpretable concepts \cite{li2024emergentworldrepresentationsexploring, karvonen2024emergentworldmodelslatent, McGrath_2022}.
In practice, this involves constructing a dataset in where each input is annotated with the presence or absence of a given concept.
Each input is then projected into the \latentSpaceAcronym of a deep learning model, and a simple classifier (most often linear) is trained in a supervised manner to predict the presence of the concept from the latent representations.
If the classifier succeeds, this indicates that the latent representation contains information relevant to that concept, suggesting that the network may rely on this information when making decisions.
While probing provides valuable insights into the internal structure of \latentSpaceAcronym, it cannot reveal novel concepts and relies heavily on manually labeled data.

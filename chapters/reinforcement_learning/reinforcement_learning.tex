\chapter{Reinforcement Learning}
\label{chap:reinforcement_learning}

This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of reinforcement learning, providing the necessary foundation for the ideas developed throughout the rest of this manuscript.  
This chapter can be seen as a synthesis of the standard references in reinforcement learning, in particular the works of Sutton and Barto \cite{sutton2018reinforcement}.  
Its structure is as follows:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Historical developments: an overview of the key ideas and milestones that led to the emergence of reinforcement learning as a unified field.
    \item Markov Decision Processes (MDPs): a formalization of how reinforcement learning problems are structured and modeled.
    \item Actor and critic roles: a presentation of the respective roles of the policy (actor) and value estimation (critic).
    \item Actor–critic learning: an explanation of how the actor and critic are jointly trained through interaction with the environment.
    \item Policy evaluation: a discussion of how learned policies are assessed and compared.
\end{itemize}
